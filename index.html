Breaking Down Grocery Shopping

Breaking Down Grocery Shopping Content

Grocery shopping is not a single task

It's actually a complex series of steps involving executive functioning, sensory processing, decision-making, budgeting, time management, and physical demands.

Let's break it down into steps so we can identify specific barriers.

Step 1: Assessment & Planning

  • What food do I need?
  • What do I already have?
  • What's my budget?
  • When can I go? How much time do I have?
  • What's my capacity level today?
  • Do I need to make a list?

Step 2: Preparation

  • Getting to the store (transportation, energy, time)
  • Bringing bags, list, payment method
  • Mental preparation for sensory environment
  • Timing (avoiding peak hours? Need to go now?)

Step 3: The Shopping Experience

  • Navigating the sensory environment
  • Making decisions in the moment
  • Dealing with decision fatigue
  • Managing overwhelm and time blindness
  • Social interactions (staff, other shoppers)
  • Sticking to budget while making choices

Step 4: Checkout & Transport

  • Waiting in line
  • Social interaction with cashier
  • Bagging decisions
  • Getting groceries to vehicle/home
  • Physical demands of carrying

Step 5: Putting Away & Recovery

  • Unloading groceries
  • Organizing in fridge/pantry
  • Dealing with remaining energy/capacity
  • Processing the experience

Reflection: What Are Your Barriers?

Reflection: What Are Your Barriers Content

Personal Reflection

Looking at the 5 steps above, take a moment to identify which steps feel most challenging for you.

Consider:

  • Which step do you avoid or procrastinate the most?
  • Which step drains your energy the fastest?
  • Are there steps that sometimes feel manageable and other times feel impossible?
  • What emotions arise at each step?

Remember: Barriers are not personal failings. They are information about what supports you need.

"Your capacity is not constant. Your needs are not failures. Your accommodations are not luxuries."
